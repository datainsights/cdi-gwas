Q&A 3 What are the essential components of a GWAS dataset?

3.1 Explanation

After downloading a GWAS dataset, it‚Äôs important to understand its structure before proceeding with analysis.

A typical GWAS dataset includes two types of information:

  1. Genotypic Data
    • Markers (SNPs): Represent genetic variants across samples.
    • Chromosome: Indicates where each SNP is located.
    • Base Position: Genomic coordinate of each SNP.
    • These are usually stored in PLINK files: .ped, .map, or .bed/.bim/.fam.
  2. Phenotypic Data
    • Trait values for each sample (e.g., height, yield, disease resistance).
    • Often stored in a tabular file with columns like FID, IID, and one or more trait columns.

In this guide, we use the rice diversity panel from Zhao et al., 2011 which provides:

  • üìë Phenotype File: RiceDiversity_44K_Phenotypes_34traits_PLINK.txt
  • The files are now renamed and saved under data/ with a rice_gwas_ prefix.
